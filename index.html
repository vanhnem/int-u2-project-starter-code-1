The Issue of Period Poverty

So, What is 'Period Poverty?'

Period poverty refers to the lack of access to menstrual products and hygiene education.

Countries

Around the world, approximately 2 billion people go through a menstrual cycle each month, but they do not always have access to the resources they need, especially in developing countries.

  • In many developing countries, menstrual products are unaffordable for low-income families.
  • In Nepal, 72% of adolescent girls spent the majority of their time in menstrual huts during their periods.
  • In Thailand, menstrual products typically cost women 12% of the daily minimum wage and were eventually classified as luxury items, with tax rates reaching 40%.

Mental/Physical Health

In some cultures, women are thought to be isolated and denied entry to places of worship because of "uncleanliness," and this difference of treatment causes them to feel unwanted and suppressed. Such treatment could easily lead to people spiraling into mental health issues and the like.

Stigma/Discrimination

Despite its prevalence, there still exists a sense of secrecy about the topic of menstrual cycles. In classrooms, the act of teaching about it is considered taboo. In Egypt, a country home to ~60 million women, 66% of girls were unaware of menstrual cycles until they experienced it themselves. In cultural contexts, women are "seen as dirty or untouchable, restricting their movement and access to spaces." However, if we are able to move past these misconceptions about periods, products that are vital to the hygienic state of many around the globe will become more accessible.
